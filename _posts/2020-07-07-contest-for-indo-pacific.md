This book was introduced through one of its [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GVeU83X72J0).

### What's the book is about?
It's a nonfiction book on Indo-Pacific, an idea or a geopolitical region, written by Rory Medcalf. The author is a [Professor](https://researchers.anu.edu.au/researchers/medcalf-r) has been Head of the National Security College (NSC) at the Australian National University.

### Contents

The book's full title is Contest for the Indo-Pacific: Why **China** Won't Map the Future. So, obviously this book revolves around the nation.

**CHAPTER 1. Of Names, Maps and Power** This chapter details us about how did the term Indo-Pacific came into existence. Who coined the term, who are all the stack-holders of the region and significance of the region.

**CHAPTER 2. A Submerged History of Asia** This chapter travels across the time to tell us who *was* the leader of the region from starting of this millennium to how the European saw the region, how the 2 great wars shaped the nations and how the China behaves very similar to the European colonisers. It touches the empires - Chola, Srivijaya, Ming Dynasty, General - Zheng He, philosophers - Chanakya, Sun Tzu, Rabindranath Tagore.

**CHAPTER 3. Odyssey of Nations: The Quest for a Regional Home** This chapter covered the importance of Asian unity thorough [Asian Relations Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_Relations_Conference) by listing the representatives from various regions of Asia.  There is a quote mentioned in this chapter,
> I had a selfish motive in wanting India to emerge... If India doesn't emerge, Asia will be submerged  - Lee Kuan Yew, Singapore's founding leader

The chapter ends with the formation of [ASEAN + Six](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_Relations_Conference) bringing India, Australia & New Zealand into the sphere.
