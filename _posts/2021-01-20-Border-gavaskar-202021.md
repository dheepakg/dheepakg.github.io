We always love stories involving an underdog winning over the establishment. The David vs. Goliath theme is prevalent all over the history spread across the civilizations - the Kurukshetra war, Hercules's 12 _impossible_ labors, or the epic of Gilgamesh. Even in our contemporary period, we love to hear stories of a similar theme, from Sherlock vs. Moriarty to James Bond, stopping harmful acts by terrorists to Avengers, stopping Thanos's mad world-conquering run.

WWE - the popular sports entertainment follows the same theme. The storylines are made to showcase rivalry - _feud_ between _face_ and _heel_, portraying _face_ as underpowered against _heel_ who wins the matches by hook or crook. And the crowd enjoys the victory of _face_ over _heel_.

Calling Cricket a complex sport may not be wrong. There are three different versions of the game played across the nations - T20, ODI & Test matches and far [more versions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forms_of_cricket) played at club level. The Test match is the longest one spanning five days, an attritional sport nonetheless. The result can be won, loss, draw or tie. Not to ignore weather too plays a big role, rain could result in curtailing the match duration.

T20 is the shortest format, result-oriented, because of the glamour involved, its the most popular one among the masses.

The Test match enjoys being the toughest format status as there are more ways to avoid losses by playing for _draw_ or through _interferring weather_. To give a perspective since the start of playing Test matches, one in three-match ended in Draw, implying no winner even after toiling for five long days<sup>*</sup>.

Against all the odds, the Indian team rose victorious after a month-long attritional series. Winning a series is unarguably a big task, but **_why_** it's considered the _greatest_ ever victory?


  **1. The unsettled team became the winner**

The players got injured even before the start of the series. And, the pattern continued throughout the tournament. There were a total of 20 players played in the series. All the permutations and combinations were employed to form a playing XI. Eventually, 2 of the net bowlers debuted in the fourth test.

**3. The young turks did the unconventional**

Every one of us thought the franchise cricket is the one to kill the Tests. Contrary to that, some of the well-known products of IPL injected much-needed adrenaline into the crawling Test matches, turning it into a spectacle.

Barring the first match, players debuted in all three tests. Gill debuted as the opener to the team that got bowled out to 36. Siraj debuted in place of Shami, who got his wrist fractured. Navdeep Saini in place of Umesh. Washington Sundar in place of Jadeja and Nattu instead of Bumrah.

At the end of the series, Gill turned out to be one of the top 5 run-getters of the series, with a match-defining 90s in the final test. Siraj became the senior bowler of the team in the final test and took 5-fer. Washington Sundar and Shardul Thakur put on a partnership to reduce the deficit, and Sundar contributed an IPL-ish innings to chase down a mammoth target of 328.

When the _core_ group played as a rock to stabilize the batting, Rishab Pant, another product of IPL, scored back-to-back 90s in the 4th innings of 3rd and 4th innings for a draw and a win, respectively.


**4. The uneven contest**

The result of the final test is a _black swan_ event. The Gabba is the venue. It is a fortress for the Australians, who were having an undefeated run for a long time in the ground. For Indians, the entire bowling unit needed an overhaul. The key bowlers - Ashwin and Bumrah, joined the injured list and pulled out of the final test.

On the bouncy pitch of Gabba, the Australians presented the best bowling combination, with cumulative 1000+ wickets. On the other hand, Indian bowling was too depleted and roped in the net bowlers into the playing XI. Sundar & Nattu debuted, Nattu never played an FC, and Sundar last played an FC 3 years back. The other two were, Shardul and Siraj. Shardul's debut ended in 3 balls, and effectively it's a debut match. For Siraj, it's his 3rd match. And so, he is the leader of the bowling unit.


**5. Off-field drama**

Before the series begins, the 'experts' projected a strong performance by the hosts, if not a decent performance against a not-so-strong touring side. The _36 all-out_ in Adelaide justified the projections.

Living in the bubble for a prolonged duration has some effects on mental abilities. As if it's not enough, the racist behavior of some idiotic fans. Raising COVID numbers threatening the change in venue of final tests. The Australian players chirping into Indian ears in the name of banter, and some of it captured in stump mic and going around in social media.

Amidst all this drama, a team composed of more rookies than seasoned campaigners pulled the unthinkable. And so calling this series as all-time greatest is not at all an exaggeration.
