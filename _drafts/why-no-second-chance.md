It all started back in 2013. This is from the ruling UPA government, which has navigated the country from the 2008 recession. But UPA-II was polar opposite of the UPA-I. Even there is a book on how India has [lost a decade](https://www.amazon.in/Lost-Decade-2008-18-Devolved-Without-ebook/dp/B07QLY4562). Adding to the lost opportunity was the series of scams, resulted in a strong anti-incumbency wave going through the nation.

Then came the Narendra Modi, riding this big wave. There was even a term - [Modi wave](https://www.rediff.com/news/column/explaining-the-modi-wave/20140606.htm). There was quite a long list of [promises from the BJP (pdf)](http://cdn.narendramodi.in/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Manifesto2014highlights.pdf), some of them were unattainable like building 100 smart cities. Modi being a crowd puller, the millions voted for him, there was a landslide vistory for the right-wing party, putting the INC into historical low, losing 162 of the constituency compared with 2009 election.

Even though, there were few things went right, what has changed in this 5 year tenure that went against the ruling government?

#### Not reaching out to the public
The basic expectation from any goverment is its accountability. The leader is expected to explaing his decision to the public. That is the way to go for the democracy. Modi was active in social media platforms even before becoming the PM, and even after becoming the PM he's active in twitter. But there is huge population which has no digital foot prints. To address this crowd the PM chose Radio, this has a good reach across the country. But its one-way communication. He can talk as much as he wants, there is no way to question his action by the press. As of writing this down (19th May), there was one press meet. Even then he didn't took a question.


#### Centralisation of power
Its not an exaggeration if we say Modi has single-handedly won the 2014 election. But its the team which matters. As you can see the above paragraph, there is too much emphasize on Modi. The goverment is a big machine with lots of cogs, the PM being a biggest cog. But in case of this government, everything is done by the PM. He takes credit for each and every schemes put forward by the ministries. There is no number 2 in the goverment (Amit Shah is a party leader, not part of government).


#### Mixing politics & religion
The BJP is a right-wing party. Even though the court has acquitted him, Modi has a bad impression over how he handles '02 riots. There were lots of such cases. Padmavati becoming [Padmavat](https://thewire.in/film/padmavati-release-delayed-sanjay-leela-bhansali-deepika-padukone), Ghar wapsi and love jihad concept, dragging [mythological beliefs into Science conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Indian_Science_Congress_ancient_aircraft_controversy), cow vigilante and crowd lynching and so on. From the ancient Rome to present time middle east, we are seeing how worse things could go when politics and religion mixed up together. Yet, we are not learning lesson.


#### Loose canons among the party
The leaders in the party are not matured enough for the bigger stage. With the party being a right wing, the statements from their leaders were too uncivilized. Targetting the minority communities in several instances. There is even an article titled [‘Prostitute’ and 7 Other Disturbing Remarks by BJP Leaders](https://www.bloombergquint.com/politics/controversial-statements-embarrassed-bjp-sadhvi-prachi-sakshi-maharaj-yogi-adityanath-haraamzaade-prostitute). These leaders not some chap roaming in hamlet. They were selected by the people. As the younger generation is watching them, the right behaviour is necessary aspect.

#### Fanatics justifying every mishap
Along with the party workers, fanatics contribute every political party. The fanatics justify each and every step of their goverment, even if it defies every logic under the sun. There are cases where the hate spewing twitter accounts were [followed by none other than the PM](https://www.nytimes.com/2017/09/08/world/asia/india-modi-twitter.html). Following an account may not be seen a big deal. But it acts a encouragement for these trolls. The crazy crowd once activated, it's tough to bring herd-mentality under control, posing a threat to peaceful society.

 The PM is too powerful to be questioned, leaders dont think before making some ugly comments, lack of feedback loop between rulers and the one thats been ruled by, crazy crowd going scot free even after causing communal comments. These are not a sign of good team or an administration.
